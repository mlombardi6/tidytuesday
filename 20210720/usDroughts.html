Data

2001-2021 US droughts data from the Drought Monitor.

The U.S. Drought Monitor is jointly produced by the National Drought Mitigation Center at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, the United States Department of Agriculture, and the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration. Map courtesy of NDMC.
drought levels
levels labels
None no drought
D0 abnormally dry
D1 moderate drought
D2 severe drought
D3 extreme drought
D4 exceptional drought

 

Droughts cycles

Dry and wet cycles are dependent on geography and climate drivers. Drought phases in the US can be explained by changes in the track of extratropical cyclones, which can occur during climate cycles such as the El Niño-Southern Oscillation, or ENSO, as well as the North Atlantic Oscillation, Pacific Decadal Oscillation, and Atlantic multidecadal oscillation. The country’s contiguous western and especially southwestern region has experienced widespread drought since about year 2000 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Droughts_in_the_United_States).
